About the Balkan Peninsula

The Balkan Peninsula is a region in southeastern Europe that comprises several countries. It is known for its rich history, diverse cultures, and scenic landscapes.

Balkan Peninsula Countries

The term “Balkan Countries”, or simply “The Balkans”, refers to the countries that are located on the Balkan Peninsula, which is situated in southeastern Europe. There is, however, a distinction between what constitutes the Balkan Countries from a geographical perspective and what constitutes them from a political perspective. In terms of geography, the boundaries of the Balkan Peninsula in the north are generally considered to be the Danube and Sava rivers. Thus, from a geographical perspective, the Balkan Peninsula includes most of Slovenia, southeastern Romania, Croatia, Serbia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, North Macedonia, Albania, Montenegro, Bulgaria, continental Greece, and the European part of Turkey. In terms of politics, however, Greece and Turkey are normally excluded from the discourse of Balkan affairs.

  • Albania
  • Bosnia and Herzegovina
  • Bulgaria
  • Croatia
  • Greece
  • Macedonia (North Macedonia)
  • Montenegro
  • Romania
  • Serbia
  • Slovenia
  • Turkey (European part)

Religions in the Balkan Countries

The Balkan countries have a diverse religious landscape. The three dominant religions in the region are:

  • Eastern Orthodox Christianity
  • Roman Catholicism
  • Islam

Membership in International Organizations

Most of the Balkan countries are now members of the European Union (EU). Additionally, some countries are also members of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO).

Geography of the Balkan Peninsula

The Balkan Peninsula is bordered by various bodies of water and landforms:

  • To the west: Adriatic Sea
  • To the southwest: Ionian Sea
  • To the south: Aegean Sea and the Mediterranean Sea
  • To the east: Black Sea
  • To the southeast: Dardanelles and Bosporus Straits (separating the peninsula from the Anatolian part of Turkey)
